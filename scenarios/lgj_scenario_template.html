2–2.5 hrs
One sentence that captures what this scenario is about. The tagline the GM reads aloud first, before anything else. Kept to a single line or two. Should feel evocative, not explanatory.
The question
The single question players are asked to wrestle with over the session. Usually phrased as an open "how" or "can we" — not a yes/no. Example: How do we live well with what returns to us? or Can a community survive without sacrificing its weakest member?

The world

A paragraph of geography and economy. Where is this? What is the land like? Who lives there? What do they do? Keep it evocative but concrete — the GM reads this aloud at the top of the session and players should be able to picture it.

A paragraph of the present moment. What has just happened, or what is happening as play begins? What is the pressure the world is under? This is where the question of the scenario becomes personal — why are we here, now?

Variant
An optional second victory condition for a replay, or for a differently-motivated table.

Debrief prompts

Three to five questions specific to this scenario. Ask them at the end of the session, after the 15 minutes of general debrief.

  1. A question that asks players to connect their in-game choices to the real question the scenario posed.
  2. A question that surfaces where the table felt stuck or surprised.
  3. A question that asks what they'd do differently if they played again.
  4. (Optional) A question tied to the scenario's real-world referent.
  5. (Optional) A question that invites the table to name what's missing — from the scenario, from the world.
